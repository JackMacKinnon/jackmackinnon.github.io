Personal Fitness and Health

05/12-Present

I have been into personal fitness now for pretty much all of my teenage years and early adult life. It really is like a physical form of therapy.

My brother first introduced me to personal training and personal fitness when I was nearing the end of my freshman year in high school. At this point in my life I was getting more serious into competitive soccer, playing for the top-level rep team in my area. My brother had done a few summers at a performance gym for hockey training (he was also playing top-level rep hockey for our city), so he had an idea of what he was doing.

He showed me some basics and instilled enough motivation in me to keep up with it, and before long, I was hooked. I just felt better when I exercised; so I did it daily. When the potency of my workouts wasn't what it once was, I did some Googling and came up with new exercises and variations to make them more effective. I also came away with some knowledge of human kinesiology that I applied to my own regiment.

I got bigger, stronger, faster, and it showed. I was better at my sport because of it; but more importantly, I became a new person. I wasn't the wimpy, insecure, always slouching kid I once was. I started to like who I was, and it felt good.

Exercise changed my life for the better, so I continue to do it to this day.

Photography

05/16-Present

My most recent hobby I've picked up is photography.

I've always loved to photograph things, and I think I can attribute that to how I find beauty in subtleties. I remember when I was young I would play with my uncle's digital camera and photograph random things around my grandmothers house, in facination.

I put my interest in photography on the backburner as I moved through my adolescent life. It wasn't until I worked a summer at Toyota Motor Manufacturing Canada that I had enough money to buy a real DSLR camera and dive into photography. I needed something to think/fantasize about while I was on the line to distract myself afterall.

Over the years, I almost attached photography to the cottage of one of my best friends, Devon. I could sit around and babble about his cottage, well, rather his old cottage, but I dont think I'm able to convey how much that place means to me. And to be quite frank, that's something I may want to keep for myself. But check out the images attached to this section, it's quite literally, now, a Canadian National Park.
